# ERASMUS IN MALTA
## Practical Tips for Exchange Students

> Make your stay safe, enjoyable and unforgettable!

---

## 1. Accommodation
**Apply early for student housing.**  
Rents are high and flats are small, so a room in a university dorm can be cheaper and it helps you meet other international students.

---

## 2. Language & Culture
**Use English, respect Maltese.**  
English is an official language alongside Maltase, but learning a few phrases shows respect:  
**Bonġu** – good morning • **Bonswa** – good evening • **Ċaw** – hi/bye • **Grazzi** – thank you.

---

## 3. University Life
**Take advantage of the international environment.**  
Many courses are taught in English and there are students from different countries. Work in groups, ask questions and join university events.

---

## 4. Daily Life & Food
**Try local flavours.**  
Maltese cuisine mixes Italian and British influences. Taste local drinks like **Kinnie** and be open to traditional dishes. Some of them you might consider strange, but we assure you, they are delicious and worth a try.

---

## 5. Night Life & Safety
**Enjoy Paceville, but stay smart.**  
Paceville is the main party district with many clubs and bars. Always go out with friends but watch your alcohol intake and be careful with gambling, even if it is legal.

---

## 6. Money Matters
**Plan a realistic budget.**  
Malta uses the **euro** currency. Check your bank card fees or consider a local account. Use your Erasmus grant wisely for rent, food, transport and free time.

---

## 7. Health & Documents
**Bring the right documents.**  
Take your ID/passport and your **European Health Insurance Card (EHIC / EKUZ)**. It makes getting medical help much easier if you need it. It's better to be safe then sorry.

---

## 8. Help from senior students
**Ask older students ("buddies") for support.**  
They already know Malta and the university, so do not be afraid to ask them for help:
- If you **lose your ID or student card**, they can go with you to the International Office or police, help you call your embassy and even lend you some money for basic needs.
- If you **feel very homesick**, they can invite you to group activities, listen to you, help you organise regular video calls with family and show you where to find counselling services.
- They help you join student groups and events (WhatsApp, ESN, university clubs), so you can make friends faster.

---

## 9. Places worth visiting
**Use weekends for small trips.**  
Visit places like **Valletta** and **Mdina** for history and views, and plan a boat trip to **Gozo** or the **Blue Lagoon on Comino** to enjoy the sea.

---

### Remember
Be open, talk to locals and other students, and say "yes" to new experiences – that is what Erasmus in Malta is all about!

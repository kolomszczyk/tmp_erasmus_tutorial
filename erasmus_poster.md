# ERASMUS IN MALTA
## Practical Tips for Exchange Students

> Make your stay safe, enjoyable and unforgettable!

---

## 1. Accommodation
**Apply early for student housing.**  
Rents are high and flats are small, so a room in a residence can be cheaper and it helps you meet other international students.

---

## 2. Language & Culture
**Use English, respect Maltese.**  
English is an official language, but learning a few Maltese phrases shows respect:  
**Bonġu** – good morning • **Bonswa** – good evening • **Ċaw** – hi/bye • **Grazzi** – thank you.

---

## 3. University Life
**Take advantage of the international environment.**  
Many courses are taught in English and there are students from different countries. Work in groups, ask questions and join university events.

---

## 4. Daily Life & Food
**Try local flavours.**  
Maltese cuisine mixes Italian and British influences. Taste local drinks like **Kinnie** and be open to traditional dishes.

---

## 5. Night Life & Safety
**Enjoy Paceville, but stay smart.**  
Paceville is the main party district with many clubs and bars. Always go out with friends, watch your alcohol intake and be careful with gambling, even if it is legal.

---

## 6. Money Matters
**Plan a realistic budget.**  
Malta uses the **euro**. Check your bank card fees or consider a local account. Use your Erasmus grant wisely for rent, food, transport and free time.

---

## 7. Health & Documents
**Bring the right documents.**  
Take your ID/passport and your **European Health Insurance Card (EHIC / EKUZ)**. It makes getting medical help much easier if you need it.

---

### Remember
Be open, talk to locals and other students, and say "yes" to new experiences – that is what Erasmus in Malta is all about!

8. Global English vs local identity

In countries where English is widely spoken as a second language, local varieties are emerging. Should these new varieties be accepted as legitimate forms of English?

GROUP TASK – Erasmus

Work in small groups to prepare practical guidelines for students who are going on an Erasmus exchange to either Ireland or Malta. Your goal is to help future exchange students avoid problems and make the most of their experience.

1. Form groups of 3–4 students.
2. Imagine a group of students from your university will soon travel to Ireland or Malta for their Erasmus exchange.
3. Your group must prepare a short set of guidelines (5–8 tips) to help them prepare for their stay.
4. After your discussion, present your advice to the class (2–3 minutes per group).

You may discuss and include tips about:

[Reference 123]

• Accommodation – finding housing, sharing flats, dealing with landlords
• Cultural differences – local customs, politeness, communication style
• University life – teaching style, expectations, group work
• Language – accents, slang, communication with locals
• Daily life – food, shopping, transport, cost of living
• Social life – making friends, joining clubs, student activities
• Travel opportunities – places worth visiting during the exchange
• Practical advice – documents, banking, weather, safety
